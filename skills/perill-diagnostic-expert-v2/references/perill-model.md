# PERILL Model Documentation

## Six Dimensions

### P — Purpose and Motivation (目的与动机)
The team's reason for being. Indicators include clarity of shared vision, goals, priorities, and alignment between individual and collective identity. High performance: clear mission linked with wider purpose, goal clarity, role clarity, high energy, willingness to put team first. Dysfunction: vague purpose, conflicting priorities, personal agendas predominate.

### E — External System and Process (外部系统与流程)
How the team interrelates with stakeholders (customers, suppliers, senior management). Includes managing conflicting expectations, obtaining resources, maintaining reputation. High performance: strong radar for threats/opportunities, high stakeholder reputation, clear communication. Dysfunction: reputational issues, lack of resources, political environment, unclear stakeholder expectations.

### R — Relationship (关系)
How members work together — enjoyment, mutual respect, psychological safety, honest feedback, genuine concern. High performance: complementary strengths, high levels of honest feedback, positive conflict, psychological safety. Dysfunction: conflict denial, lack of psychological safety, cliques, blame culture.

### I — Internal System and Process (内部系统与流程)
Internal mirror to external — workflow management, mutual support, communication quality, work design, decision-making. High performance: distributed leadership, strong decision-making, role clarity, rapid innovation. Dysfunction: recurring quality problems, unclear roles, inadequate review systems, unclear decision processes.

### L — Learning (学习)
Team's ability to respond to changing environment and maintain continuous improvement. Includes performance enhancement, capability building, capacity development. High performance: team development plans, positive attitude to mistakes, habit of reflection, seeking feedback. Dysfunction: "too busy" syndrome, insufficient learning value, resistance to change, repeated mistakes.

### Ld — Leadership (领导力)
Emphasizes leadership as a function, not just the leader. How team and designated leader work together to ensure leadership happens. High performance: distributed leadership, leader as role model, leader provides moral direction, open conversations. Dysfunction: absentee or over-controlling leadership, power concentrated in leader, lack of open dialogue.

## Systemic Principle

All six dimensions interact. A problem in one dimension may be driven by issues in others. Linear solutions fail because any change in one part stimulates the system to revert. The model enables teams to address issues systemically rather than linearly.

## Dimension Interconnections (Examples)

Common causal patterns:
- I → E: Internal process breakdown causes external collaboration failure
- P → R: Unclear purpose creates relationship tension
- Ld → I: Leadership style shapes internal processes
- E → P: External misalignment erodes team purpose
- R → L: Poor relationships block collective learning
- L → I: Learning gaps manifest in process deficiencies
